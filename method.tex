\section{Method}
Inspired by Integrated Gradients (IG) \citep{sundararajan2017axiomatic}, we
estimate step contribution by applying path-integral attribution to a lightweight
trajectory-level scorer trained from final rewards.
This avoids relying on an external strong value model or process judge:
the scorer is learned internally from outcome-labeled trajectories, and its
gradients provide a dense signal for how each reasoning step changes the
trajectory score.

IG is particularly suitable for our cooperative-game view because, in the
differentiable path-attribution setting, it instantiates the Aumann--Shapley
value, the continuous cost-sharing analogue of the Shapley value
\citep{shapley1953value,aumann1974values,sundararajan2017axiomatic}.
Its completeness property preserves the allocation interpretation:
step attributions sum to the scorer difference between the observed trajectory
and a baseline.

SPRM builds on this attribution signal with three components.
First, the local channel uses IG to assign trajectory-internal credit to
individual reasoning steps.
Second, a causal-consistency filter retains local labels only when gradient
attribution agrees with counterfactual evidence from step masking.
Third, the global channel performs reward reshaping with a cross-trajectory
semantic prior, so steps that appear in more successful neighborhoods receive
additional support.
These signals are distilled into a dual-head process reward model (PRM).

\subsection{Trajectory Scorer and Local Credit Assignment}
\label{sec:method_local}

For each trajectory, we encode the question and steps as
\(
X_i=[x_{i,0},x_{i,1},\ldots,x_{i,T_i}]
\in\mathbb{R}^{(T_i+1)\times d}
\),
where \(x_{i,0}\) is the question-end representation and \(x_{i,t}\) represents
step \(s_{i,t}\).
We train a lightweight differentiable trajectory scorer
\(F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}}:\mathbb{R}^{(T_i+1)\times d}\rightarrow[0,1]\)
from final rewards only:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:stage1_objective}
\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star
:=
\arg\min_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}}
\mathbb{E}_{(\tau,r)\sim\mathcal{D}}
\left[
\mathrm{BCE}\!\left(F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}}(\mathrm{encoder}(\tau)),r\right)
\right].
\end{equation}

Local credit assignment attributes the scorer output to individual steps.
Let \(b\in\mathbb{R}^{d}\) be a semantically empty baseline, let
\(B_i=[b,\ldots,b]\), and define
\(\gamma_i(\alpha)=B_i+\alpha(X_i-B_i)\).
The Aumann--Shapley/IG credit of step \(s_{i,t}\) is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:delta_local}
\delta_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}
:=
\left(x_{i,t}-b\right)^\top
\int_0^1
\nabla_{x_{i,t}}
F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}\!\left(\gamma_i(\alpha)\right)
\,d\alpha .
\end{equation}
By IG completeness, the attributions over all sequence positions decompose
\(F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(X_i)
-F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(B_i)\)
\citep{sundararajan2017axiomatic,pmlr-v119-sundararajan20b};
we use the reasoning-step positions as local credits.

To make this marginal-contribution signal sensitive to interactions among
reasoning steps, we compute a counterfactual interaction score by masking step
features.
Let \(X_i^{(-t)}\) replace \(x_{i,t}\) with \(b\), and let
\(X_i^{(-t,-u)}\) replace both \(x_{i,t}\) and \(x_{i,u}\).
\begin{align}
\label{eq:direct_counterfactual}
R_{i,t}^{\mathrm{dir}}
&=
F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(X_i)
-
F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(X_i^{(-t)}), \\
\label{eq:pairwise_interaction}
R_{i,(t,u)}^{\mathrm{int}}
&=
F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(X_i)
-
F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(X_i^{(-t)})
-
F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(X_i^{(-u)})
+
F_{\theta_{\mathrm{scorer}}^\star}(X_i^{(-t,-u)}).
\end{align}
For a local window
\(\mathcal{W}(t)=\{u \mid 1 \le u < t,\; t-u \le w\}\),
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pairwise_counterfactual}
R_{i,t}^{\mathrm{causal}}
:=
R_{i,t}^{\mathrm{dir}}
+
\beta
\frac{
\sum_{u\in\mathcal{W}(t)} \max\!\big(0, R_{i,(t,u)}^{\mathrm{int}}\big)\,
R_{i,(t,u)}^{\mathrm{int}}
}{
\sum_{u\in\mathcal{W}(t)} \max\!\big(0, R_{i,(t,u)}^{\mathrm{int}}\big)
+ \epsilon
}.
\end{equation}
\(R_{i,t}^{\mathrm{causal}}\) combines the direct counterfactual effect of step
\(t\) with positive pairwise interactions from nearby previous steps.
It is used to align marginal-contribution labels with interaction-aware
counterfactual evidence: only steps that are consistently positive or
consistently negative under both \(\delta_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}\) and
\(R_{i,t}^{\mathrm{causal}}\) are used for local supervision.

\subsection{Global Reward-Reshaping Prior}
\label{sec:method_global}

The global channel is not a second credit-assignment rule.
It is a reward-reshaping prior that stabilizes local supervision by asking
whether semantically similar steps across trajectories are associated with
success.
For step representation \(x_{i,t}\), retrieve top-\(M\) neighbors from other
trajectories and denote them by \(\mathcal{H}_M(i,t)\).
With similarity weights \(\omega_{i,t,j,\ell}\ge 0\), define
\[
\hat r_{i,t}
:=
\frac{
\sum_{(j,\ell)\in\mathcal{H}_M(i,t)} \omega_{i,t,j,\ell} r_j
}{
\sum_{(j,\ell)\in\mathcal{H}_M(i,t)} \omega_{i,t,j,\ell}
}.
\]
Comparing this neighborhood success rate with the dataset average
\(
\bar r=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} r_i
\)
gives the global signal
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:delta_global}
\delta_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}}
:=
\hat r_{i,t}-\bar r .
\end{equation}
Positive \(\delta_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}}\) means that the step pattern appears in
more successful neighborhoods than average.
This cross-trajectory prior reshapes the final step reward away from noisy
single-trajectory evidence.

\subsection{PRM Training and Inference}
\label{sec:method_prm}

The PRM uses a shared backbone with local and global heads,
parameterized by \(\theta_{\mathrm{PRM}}\).
For step \(s_{i,t}\), the heads predict
\(\hat y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}\in(0,1)\) and
\(\hat y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}}\in(0,1)\).
We binarize the constructed signals as
\[
y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}=\mathbf{1}\!\left(\delta_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}>0\right),
\qquad
y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}}=\mathbf{1}\!\left(\delta_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}}>0\right).
\]
The local label is used only when attribution and counterfactual signs agree:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:local_mask}
m_{i,t}
:=
\mathbf{1}\!\left(
\delta_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}} \cdot R_{i,t}^{\mathrm{causal}} > 0
\right).
\end{equation}
The final objective is
\begin{align}
\label{eq:stage3_objective}
\theta_{\mathrm{PRM}}^\star
&:=
\arg\min_{\theta_{\mathrm{PRM}}}
\mathbb{E}_{(\tau_i,r_i)\sim\mathcal{D}}
\Big[
\sum_{t=1}^{T_i}
m_{i,t}\,
\mathrm{BCE}\!\left(\hat y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}, y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}\right)
\notag\\
&\quad+
\sum_{t=1}^{T_i}
\mathrm{BCE}\!\left(\hat y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}}, y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}}\right)
\Big].
\end{align}
At inference time, the final step reward is
\[
\hat y_{i,t}
:=
\lambda \hat y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{loc}}
+
(1-\lambda)\hat y_{i,t}^{\mathrm{glo}},
\]
where \(\lambda\in[0,1]\) controls the trade-off between trajectory-local credit
and global reward reshaping.
