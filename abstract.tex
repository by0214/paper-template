\begin{abstract}
Process reward models (PRMs) are increasingly important for large language
model reasoning, but existing PRM supervision pipelines typically rely on
human annotations, Monte Carlo rollouts, or external LLM judges.
We propose SPRM, a Shapley-inspired framework that learns step-level process
supervision directly from outcome-only trajectories.
SPRM combines a differentiable trajectory scorer with Aumann--Shapley-style
local attribution, a pairwise counterfactual consistency check, and a global
semantic prior derived from cross-trajectory neighbors, and distills these
signals into a dual-head PRM.
On ProcessBench and PRMBench, SPRM achieves the strongest results among
comparable baseline PRMs, reaching 60.0 average F1 on ProcessBench and 62.4
overall on PRMBench.
It also transfers effectively to Best-of-\(N\) reranking, where it improves
inference-time search quality as the candidate budget grows.
These results suggest that principled credit assignment from sparse
outcome-level rewards can provide scalable and reusable supervision for
reasoning PRMs without external step annotation.
\end{abstract}
